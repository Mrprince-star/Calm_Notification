The average phone buzzes dozens of times a day

Group chats, social media, games, promotions, delivery updates — most of it isn't urgent, but it all arrives the same way: instantly, demanding attention. Turning off notifications entirely means missing things that actually matter. Muting one app at a time is exhausting and never quite works.

Calm Notifications takes a different approach: instead of blocking notifications, it organizes them. You decide what's truly urgent. Everything else still reaches you — just grouped into a calm, scheduled summary instead of a constant stream of interruptions.

How Calm Notifications helps

Two simple categories. Total control.

⚡ Priority apps, instant

Pick the handful of apps you always want right away — calls, direct messages, whatever matters most. They come through exactly as normal, with zero delay.

🔕 Everything else, batched

Social media, games, promotions, and group chats get quietly grouped and delivered as one clean summary at times you choose — morning, lunch, evening.

🔒 Privacy by design

Calm Notifications only ever sees which app sent a notification — never the message content, title, or text. No accounts, no servers, no data leaving your device.

🔥 Streaks & daily stats

See exactly how many notifications you kept quiet today, your current streak, and which app is your most frequent interrupter — all counted honestly, nothing fabricated.

Get set up in under a minute

1

Grant Notification Access

A standard Android permission that lets the app see which app sent a notification — never the message itself. Revoke it anytime in your phone's settings.

2

Choose your Priority apps

Search your installed apps and mark the ones you need instantly. Everything else defaults to Batched automatically.

3

Set your digest times

Pick 2-4 times a day for your quiet summary. Morning, lunch, and evening work well for most people.

4

That's it

Batched apps stay silent until your next digest — one clean summary instead of a dozen separate interruptions.

Frequently asked questions

How do I stop notification overload on Android?

The most effective way is to separate notifications into two groups: apps you need instantly (calls, messages) and apps that can wait (social media, games, promotions). Calm Notifications does this automatically — mark a handful of apps as Priority, and everything else gets quietly grouped into a few daily summaries instead of interrupting you constantly.

How can I reduce WhatsApp notifications without missing important messages?

Instead of muting WhatsApp entirely, batch group chats and less urgent conversations into scheduled digests, while keeping direct messages from important contacts instant. You still see everything, just on your own schedule.

Is Calm Notifications free?

Yes, completely free to download and use, with no account required.

Does Calm Notifications read my messages?

No. It only reads which app sent a notification and the app name, to group and count them. It never reads, stores, or transmits the actual message content, title, or text.
